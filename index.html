About

I currently work as a Quantitative Analyst and Portfolio Manager, where I design and optimize systematic futures trading strategies using Python and automation-first research workflows. I also build and maintain end-to-end automated trading systems, covering research, broker-connected trading bots, and standalone strategy deployment on dedicated servers in Python and C++.

In parallel, I develop data pipelines for portfolio reporting, rebalancing, and risk analysis, replacing manual processes with reliable code.

My academic and research training is rooted in Computer Science, AI, and Robotics, including a PhD in Computer Science (Robotics) at the University of Strasbourg and a Master's in Computer Vision & Robotics (Erasmus Mundus). During this period, I worked extensively with deterministic and optimization-driven methods (including robust calibration and localization-style problems), which shaped how I build robust and explainable systems. This foundation translates directly into my ability to develop profitable AI-based trading bots and the infrastructure required to run them consistently.

Based in France, I continue exploring robotics and automation beyond finance through hands-on projects, such as an affordable insulin pump prototype, autonomous flight for RC airplanes, and home automation systems.
